# The Tells

The reference corpus. Match on the idea, not the exact string — all tenses, gerunds, plurals count. One tell is noise; a cluster is the signal. The list only grows.

---

## Filler & intensifier words

Single words that add texture without adding meaning. They feel "writerly" but are stock LLM vocabulary.

- **delve / delves into** — the most reliable single tell. Humans say "look at," "get into," "dig into."
- **intricate** — decorative adjective standing in for an actual description.
- **tapestry / rich tapestry** — almost never used by people describing real things.
- **holistic** — corporate-mystic filler.
- **synergy** — empty business-speak, especially "unlock synergy."
- **ecosystem** — fine for biology; a tell when applied to a team, a product, or "our holistic ecosystem."
- **at scale** — bolted onto verbs to sound operational ("leverage at scale").
- **leverage** (as a verb) — "use" wearing a suit.
- **robust** — vibe-adjective version, not the statistical one.
- **game-changer** — empty superlative replacing a real claim.
- **paradigm shift** — same, with a PhD costume.
- **ever-evolving / fast-paced / today's landscape** — scene-setting throat-clearing.
- **actually / genuinely / truly** — filler emphasis real editors cut.
- **such / suction filler** — "such a powerful idea," padding for weight.
- **quietly** — fake-profound adverb. "Quietly building," "quietly shipping." Adds nothing, signals a lot.
- **seamless / seamlessly** — frictionless-product vocabulary.
- **boasts** — "the app boasts a clean interface." No human says this aloud.
- **navigate / navigating** — "navigating the complexities of." Motion-flavored filler.
- **underscore / underscores** — "this underscores the importance of."
- **foster / fostering** — "fostering collaboration." Greeting-card verb.

## Announcement pauses (windup before the point)

Constructions that promise insight and then make you wait for it. They flag a thought as profound instead of just stating it.

- **Here's the thing:** — promises a payoff, delivers a colon.
- **The result?** — fake rhetorical question as a drumroll.
- **But honestly?** — manufactured candor before a non-confession.
- **What's fascinating is…** — tells you to be interested instead of being interesting.
- **What struck me was…** — same move, memoir flavor.
- **Something I keep coming back to…** — announces reflection rather than reflecting.
- **What's still sitting with me…** / **this is still sitting with me…** — the reflection-flag move. Says a thought is lingering and deep instead of just saying the thought.
- **Let me explain.** — beat to imply the prior line was a mic drop.
- **Here's what I learned.** — listicle wind-up.
- **And that changes everything.** — closing inflation, payoff never specified.

## False-contrast constructions

The signature move: manufacture depth by negating a strawman.

- **It's not X, it's Y** — the canonical form. "It's not about the slides, it's about the journey."
- **It's not just X, it's Y** — escalation variant. "Not only robust, it's a game-changer."
- **The future isn't coming, it's already here** — time-flavored template.
- **This isn't about X. It's about Y.** — same, broken into two sentences for gravity.
- **Less X, more Y** — compressed cousin. "Less hustle, more heart."

## Structural & rhythm tells

Formatting and cadence patterns, not words.

- **Em dash everywhere** — used to fake a thoughtful beat. Heavy em-dash density is a strong format tell.
- **Three-beat lists** — "faster, smarter, better." Suspiciously balanced triples.
- **One-line paragraph. For emphasis.** — isolated sentence pretending to land a punch.
- **The bold opening line + pause** — single dramatic first line, then a gap to "let it sink in." Was sharp until everyone did it.
- **Rule-of-three rhetorical escalation** — three clauses, each a notch bigger.
- **Agree? 👇 / Thoughts?** — engagement-bait close.
- **Emoji-as-punctuation** — 🚀✨🔥 standing in for tone the words don't carry.
- **Numbered "lessons" with one-line each** — "1/ ... 2/ ..." thread cadence ported into prose.

## Performance & theatre (multi-sentence)

These don't live in a single word. They show up as a *shape* across two or more sentences — the cadence of someone performing insight rather than just saying a thing. Flag the pattern, quote the span, name the move.

- **TED-talk build** — short setup, a pause, then a "revelation" delivered as if it reorders your worldview. The structure promises a profound turn the content doesn't earn.
- **The dramatic short sentence after a long one** — a flowing sentence followed by a clipped two-or-three-word line for impact. "And that's when it clicked." "Maybe so." "Let that sink in." The brevity is doing performance, not meaning.
- **The staged realization** — narrating a thought arriving in real time. "And then it hit me." "I realized something." Real insight gets stated; performed insight gets a camera angle.
- **Escalating one-line paragraphs** — three or four single-line paragraphs in a row, each a beat, building to a "point." Slide-deck rhythm imported into prose.
- **The rhetorical turn** — "But here's what nobody tells you." "And that changes everything." A pivot announced as a reveal, payoff usually vague.
- **Manufactured stakes** — small observation inflated with the gravity of a thesis. The size of the claim and the size of the language don't match.

The single-sentence pieces of this (a lone "Let that sink in.") can also be caught on their own, but the tell is strongest read as a passage. If two or three of these stack, the whole thing reads as a performance of having an idea rather than the idea.
